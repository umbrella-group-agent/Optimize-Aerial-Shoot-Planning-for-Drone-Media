DRONE-BASED AERIAL SHOOTING WORKFLOW
Platform reference: DJI Mavic 4 Pro Fly More Combo / RC 2
Purpose: Streamlined media production workflow focused on optimal flight paths, safety, and high-quality footage

======================================================================
1. MISSION OBJECTIVE
======================================================================

This workflow is designed for professional drone-based aerial shooting in media projects. It prioritizes:
- Safe and legally compliant operations
- Efficient mission execution
- High-quality, repeatable footage
- Conservative flight-path design
- Comprehensive risk identification and mitigation

Core principle:
Do not improvise critical path decisions in the air. Every important shot should be pre-structured on the ground.

======================================================================
2. OPERATIONAL PRINCIPLES
======================================================================

1. Safety overrides creative ambition.
2. Every shot must have a primary route, escape route, and abort logic.
3. Obstacle sensing is a support layer, not a substitute for conservative path planning.
4. Do not rely on advertised maximum flight times for production planning.
5. Do not fly over uninvolved people.
6. Reduce mission complexity as soon as weather, public movement, or signal quality worsens.
7. Flight paths should be designed as corridors, not lines.

======================================================================
3. PRE-PRODUCTION WORKFLOW
======================================================================

3.1 Define the mission brief
- Project name
- Location
- Date / time window
- Deliverables
- Shot list
- Target frame rate and resolution
- Camera/lens priority
- Maximum acceptable risk level
- Public exposure level
- Airspace/legal status
- Fallback non-drone alternatives

3.2 Classify the site
- Nature / wilderness
- Coastal
- Mountain
- Urban
- Industrial
- Event / public space
- Transport corridor proximity
- Water / river / harbor
- Mixed terrain

3.3 Desk reconnaissance
Check:
- Airspace restrictions
- Local drone rules
- Temporary restrictions
- Terrain maps
- Building maps
- Sun path
- Weather trend
- Wind forecast
- Likely RF interference
- Potential launch/landing zones
- Emergency alternate landing zones

3.4 Build the shot list
Classify each shot:
- Essential
- Valuable
- Optional

For each shot define:
- Camera objective
- Approximate altitude
- Direction of movement
- Lens selection
- Subject motion
- Public exposure
- Required clearance margins

======================================================================
4. ON-SITE RECONNAISSANCE
======================================================================

4.1 Walk the location before launch
Inspect for:
- Power lines
- Guy wires
- Fences
- Cranes
- Poles
- Trees
- Thin branches
- Cables over roads or water
- Antennas
- Roof edges
- Balconies
- Glass facades
- Reflective surfaces
- Narrow passages
- Water spray
- Dust
- Loose gravel
- Mud
- Slopes
- Unstable rocks
- Vehicles
- Bystanders
- Pets
- Children
- Cyclists/runners
- Bird activity
- Wildlife nesting areas

4.2 Establish operating zones
Create:
- Takeoff / landing box
- Crew zone
- Public exclusion zone
- Flight corridor
- Emergency hover area
- Escape path
- Emergency landing area

4.3 Confirm launch surface
Avoid:
- Dusty sand
- Tall grass
- Loose debris
- Standing water
- Slippery stone
- Unstable slopes

======================================================================
5. FLIGHT PATH DESIGN
======================================================================

Every shot must be planned as a corridor with:

1. Primary route
2. Escape route
3. Return route
4. Safe hover point
5. Emergency landing point
6. No-fly exclusion bubble

For each planned shot define:
- Start point
- End point
- Altitude band
- Heading/course
- Minimum lateral clearance
- Minimum vertical clearance
- Maximum speed
- Turn radius
- Wind sensitivity
- Battery launch threshold
- Battery abort threshold
- Lost-link procedure
- Public-incursion abort logic

General path rules:
- Prefer wide stand-off paths over close-proximity routes
- Avoid backward flight near obstacles
- Avoid blind turns
- Avoid low-level complex route changes
- Avoid narrow-gap flying unless strictly controlled and necessary
- Avoid relying on obstacle sensing to save poor route design

======================================================================
6. CREW STRUCTURE
======================================================================

Recommended minimum roles:
- Remote Pilot in Command
- Visual Observer
- Director / Camera Supervisor
- Ground Safety Marshal

Role priorities:
- Pilot owns aircraft safety
- Observer monitors airspace, people, and hazards
- Director focuses on visual result but cannot overrule safety
- Marshal protects sterile operating area

======================================================================
7. PRE-FLIGHT CHECKLIST
======================================================================

7.1 Legal and operational
- Airspace checked
- Permissions confirmed if required
- Local restrictions checked
- Site brief complete
- Public exposure evaluated
- Emergency procedures briefed

7.2 Aircraft
- Airframe inspected
- Propellers inspected
- Motors clear
- Gimbal clean
- Lens clean
- Battery inserted and locked
- Controller charged
- Firmware/settings verified
- Memory capacity sufficient

7.3 Flight systems
- Home point confirmed
- GNSS quality stable
- Compass status normal
- Obstacle sensing status checked
- Return-to-home behavior checked
- Flight mode verified
- Link quality checked
- Controller antenna orientation understood

7.4 Environmental
- Current wind checked on site
- Gust behavior checked
- Visibility acceptable
- No precipitation
- No active bird aggression
- Launch/landing area clean

7.5 Production
- Resolution/frame rate set
- Shutter strategy set
- White balance set
- Exposure checked
- Focus checked
- Color mode set
- Lens selected
- Shot order confirmed

======================================================================
8. EXECUTION WORKFLOW
======================================================================

8.1 Launch sequence
- Secure sterile takeoff area
- Confirm crew ready
- Lift into hover
- Hold stable hover
- Perform gentle yaw check
- Perform gentle translation check
- Perform braking check
- Confirm no abnormal drift or instability

8.2 Safety validation pass
- Fly a slow, conservative version of the route
- Validate obstacle margins
- Validate wind behavior
- Validate link quality
- Validate visibility
- Validate public exclusion line

8.3 Hero pass
- Fly only after safety pass is acceptable
- Use smooth, predictable movement
- Avoid unnecessary acceleration
- Maintain corridor discipline
- Abort immediately if conditions degrade

8.4 Variation pass
- Small changes only:
  - altitude
  - speed
  - lens
  - direction
  - framing
- Do not stack multiple complexity increases at once

8.5 Exit
- Climb or reposition into safe corridor
- Return conservatively
- Land before reserve becomes marginal

======================================================================
9. POST-FLIGHT WORKFLOW
======================================================================

- Disarm and secure aircraft
- Inspect propellers
- Inspect motors
- Inspect gimbal and lens
- Check battery temperature and remaining percentage
- Record anomalies
- Log wind behavior
- Log GNSS or signal issues
- Back up footage immediately
- Reassess next mission segment before relaunch

======================================================================
10. RISK REGISTER: DRONE PATH HAZARDS AND CONTROLS
======================================================================

10.1 Airspace and legal risks
Risks:
- Restricted zone
- Temporary restriction
- Local municipal restrictions
- Protected infrastructure proximity
- Unauthorized operations
- Flight over uninvolved people

Controls:
- Check airspace and local geo-zones before dispatch
- Confirm legal category of operation
- Redesign route away from public movement areas
- Abort if crowd density changes beyond plan

10.2 Collision risks
Risks:
- Power lines
- Guy wires
- Branches
- Cables
- Cranes
- Roof edges
- Poles
- Antennas
- Glass facades
- Narrow passages
- Blind reversals
- Backward flight into obstacles

Controls:
- Maintain generous clearance margins
- Fly slow validation pass first
- Avoid blind turns
- Avoid reverse flight near structures
- Maintain stand-off distance from facades
- Do not depend on sensors as primary defense

10.3 Weather risks
Risks:
- Sustained wind
- Gusts
- Rotor turbulence
- Downdrafts
- Updrafts
- Rain
- Mist
- Fog
- Sea spray
- Icing risk
- Hot rising air
- Reduced braking margin

Controls:
- Use local real conditions, not only app forecast
- Set separate steady wind and gust thresholds
- Reduce mission envelope in unstable weather
- Avoid cliff edges, ridges, building canyons in gusts
- Stop on first sign of precipitation or moisture contamination

10.4 Positioning risks
Risks:
- GNSS degradation
- Urban canyon multipath
- Compass disturbance
- Incorrect home point
- Drift over low-texture surfaces
- Reflective-surface confusion

Controls:
- Verify home point deliberately
- Avoid launching near steel masses or reinforced structures
- Let positioning stabilize before complex movement
- Climb first, then translate laterally

10.5 RF / link risks
Risks:
- Wi-Fi congestion
- Industrial RF interference
- Terrain masking
- Building masking
- Signal shadow behind structures
- Public event spectrum saturation

Controls:
- Maintain line-of-sight geometry
- Avoid descending behind obstructions
- Keep aircraft visible from controller position
- Define lost-link response before launch
- Shorten path in RF-heavy environments

10.6 Human exposure risks
Risks:
- Pedestrians entering corridor
- Crowd drift into takeoff zone
- Vehicles beneath route
- Cyclists/runners passing through
- Children approaching crew
- Startle reactions

Controls:
- Ground marshal controls exclusion zone
- Abort immediately on corridor breach
- Keep takeoff/landing area sterile
- Never continue the shot once a person enters hazard area

10.7 Wildlife risks
Risks:
- Birds of prey
- Territorial nesting birds
- Flocks
- Startled animals
- Protected habitats

Controls:
- Monitor bird behavior before launch
- Avoid loitering near nesting zones
- Exit immediately on bird pursuit/aggression
- Reduce operation in sensitive wildlife periods

10.8 Surface and landing risks
Risks:
- Dust ingestion
- Sand ingestion
- Grass entanglement
- Mud
- Slippery rocks
- Water edge slips
- Loose gravel
- Slope instability

Controls:
- Use landing pad or clean hard surface
- Avoid rotor wash over dust/debris
- Use only stable, dry landing zones where possible
- Reposition launch area if contamination risk is high

10.9 Battery and power risks
Risks:
- Overestimating endurance
- Cold battery performance loss
- Voltage sag
- Headwind return deficit
- Inconsistent battery condition tracking
- Mission stacking without reserve awareness

Controls:
- Use conservative endurance planning
- Set early return threshold
- Increase reserve for wind, cold, urban complexity
- Log battery health
- Do not plan around advertised maximum flight time

10.10 Camera and footage risks
Risks:
- Horizon tilt
- Focus miss
- Dirty lens
- Highlight clipping
- Flicker
- Excessive speed
- Unusable parallax
- Overuse of digital zoom
- Wrong shutter/framerate combination

Controls:
- Lock settings before mission
- Clean lens every battery cycle
- Fly slower than instinct suggests
- Use main camera for hero work unless compression is intentional
- Review first critical take before moving on

10.11 Urban-specific risks
Risks:
- Downdrafts near facades
- GNSS multipath
- Glass ambiguity
- Street canyon signal drop
- Rooftop updrafts
- Public confrontation
- Privacy complaints
- Emergency scene proximity

Controls:
- Use stand-off angles
- Avoid deep alley runs
- Avoid close facade descents
- Keep greater separation from buildings than visually seems necessary
- Avoid active emergency scenes entirely

10.12 Water/coastal risks
Risks:
- Poor depth judgement
- Spray
- Wind acceleration
- Recovery difficulty
- Low-texture visual environment

Controls:
- Raise minimum altitude
- Keep shoreline recovery option
- Avoid repeated very low passes over choppy water
- Maintain extra return reserve

10.13 Night / low-light risks
Risks:
- Reduced visibility
- Wires harder to detect
- Orientation loss
- False confidence in low-light sensing

Controls:
- Simplify route
- Increase stand-off
- Reduce speed
- Avoid low-level precision moves
- Increase abort sensitivity

12. SIMULATION A: HARSH WEATHER IN NATURE
======================================================================

Scenario:
Remote coastal or highland nature environment with strong variable wind, intermittent mist, uneven terrain, and possible bird activity.

Objective:
Capture cinematic establishing footage of cliffs, trees, or exposed terrain.

Risk profile:
High relative environmental risk.

Main risks:
- Cliff-edge turbulence
- Downdrafts on lee side
- Gust spread
- Mist reducing visibility and sensor performance
- Wet lens
- Bird aggression
- Slippery launch site
- Return deficit in headwind

Recommended path:
- Use an offset parallel route relative to the ridge or cliff
- Keep wide lateral stand-off from terrain
- Avoid crest-crossing near ridge edges
- Avoid low reveals from behind obstacles
- Avoid repeated low passes

Execution:
1. Launch from sheltered hard surface
2. Hover and test gust response
3. Fly one conservative reconnaissance pass
4. Record only stable, long-form scenic passes
5. Abort on mist deposition, unstable gusts, or bird focus
6. Land with unusually high reserve

Conclusion:
Go only as a reduced-complexity mission.
Optimal path means conservative geometry, wide margins, and no aggressive cinematic maneuvering.

======================================================================
13. SIMULATION B: NORMAL WEATHER IN URBAN AREA
======================================================================

Scenario:
Urban media shoot under normal weather with light wind and ordinary public movement.

Objective:
Capture polished establishing and detail footage of a building and surrounding streetscape.

Risk profile:
Moderate environmental risk, elevated human/structure complexity.

Main risks:
- Pedestrians entering corridor
- Vehicles beneath route
- GNSS multipath
- Building-related signal shadow
- Glass facade proximity
- Public interference
- Reduced braking margin in dense geometry

Recommended path:
- Use stand-off perimeter-based paths
- Prefer oblique approach angles
- Prefer wide orbits and controlled lateral slides
- Avoid deep canyon routes
- Avoid close facade descents
- Avoid backward flight near structures

Execution:
1. Establish sterile takeoff box
2. Ground marshal controls pedestrian exposure
3. Run slow dry pass
4. Capture high orbit / establishing shot first
5. Move to slow push-in and lateral stand-off shots
6. Re-check public movement between takes
7. Abort immediately on corridor incursion

Conclusion:
Go.
Best footage-to-risk ratio comes from stable stand-off cinematic paths, not close-proximity stunt-style flying.

======================================================================
14. HOSTILE OR CONFLICT ENVIRONMENTS
======================================================================

Do not launch media drones in military conflict zones or hostile operational environments unless the operation is formally cleared and managed under appropriate professional authority.

Automatic no-go / abort conditions:
- Active kinetic activity
- Anti-drone activity
- Jamming or spoofing indicators
- Unclear control of airspace
- Emergency or military response activity
- Inability to ensure civilian-safe operation
- Inability to prevent harm to people on the ground

Principle:
Technical flyability does not make an operation safe, lawful, or appropriate.

======================================================================
15. RECOMMENDED OPERATING DOCTRINE FOR THIS DRONE
======================================================================

For media work with the DJI Mavic 4 Pro platform:
- Use imaging strength to reduce need for close approach
- Fly slower than storyboard instinct suggests
- Treat long endurance as margin, not mission design
- Design every shot with exit and abort logic
- Use obstacle sensing as backup only
- Simplify immediately when conditions worsen
- Prioritize smooth, repeatable stand-off paths over risky close-proximity shots

======================================================================
16. FINAL SUMMARY
======================================================================

A streamlined professional drone workflow depends on:
- Strong pre-planning
- Conservative path architecture
- Clear crew roles
- Strict abort logic
- Realistic treatment of aircraft limitations
- Aggressive risk management around people, structures, weather, and signal conditions

The safest high-quality aerial workflow is not the most aggressive one.
It is the one that repeatedly delivers usable footage with predictable margins and low operational uncertainty.
